🧠 Key Concepts at a Glance

52-Week High

The highest price a stock has reached in the last 52 weeks. Stocks hitting new highs often show strong momentum and potential for continued growth.

11 Analytical Views

Different perspectives on the same data: Momentum, Trend Analysis, Value, Quality, and more. Pick what matches your investment style.

Financial Metrics

P/E ratio, ROE, Debt/Equity, and 20+ other metrics help you evaluate quality, valuation, and risk of a company.

💰 5 Investment Strategies Covered

1. Momentum Strategy
Chase recent winners hitting new highs with accelerating gains and institutional buying.
2. Quality Growth
Find companies with strong returns (ROE 15%+), low debt, growing earnings, hitting new highs = double winners.
3. Value on Pullback
Find quality companies (ROE 12%+, low debt) that are 5-50% below their 52-week high = find the dip in good companies.
4. Deep Value Strategy
Highly discounted stocks (50%+ down from high) combining recovery potential + statistical mean reversion. Higher risk/reward.
5. Hybrid Approach
Combine momentum signals with quality checks = find momentum winners that are also financially healthy (best of both worlds).

💡 Pro Tips to Get Started

  • Start with View 1 (Start Here): It gives you 11 quick metrics across all categories in one dashboard
  • Understand P/E first: It's the most important valuation metric (low P/E = cheaper, high P/E = growth expected)
  • Don't chase alone: Use multiple signals - a stock hitting a high is good, but add quality/momentum checks
  • Check the industry: View 11 shows you sector trends - some industries are on fire, others struggling
  • Time is data: Historical momentum (Δ Hits, Δ Gain%) tells you if the rally is new or fading
  • Red flags first: Check debt/equity and profit margins before falling in love with any stock
  • Export for deeper analysis: Tables are interactive - sort by any column to find your thesis in the data

❓ Common Questions

Q: Which view should I use?

It depends on your investment style. Use Quick Start section "Pick Your Investor Style" to match your approach with the right view(s).

Q: What columns do I need to pay attention to?

Start with Price, 52W High, P/E, ROE, Debt/Equity, and Momentum. Interpretation Guide explains why each matters.

Q: How do I know if a stock is a buy?

Combine multiple signals: (1) Is it on the right view for my strategy? (2) Are financial metrics good? (3) Are red flags absent? See the workflows in Interpretation Guide.

Q: What does P/E, ROE, or other terms mean?

Look them up in the Financial Glossary with examples and benchmark values for interpretation.

Q: Can I use this for long-term investing?

Yes! The app works for long-term too. Focus on quality metrics (ROE, growth, debt) and ignore short-term noise. Details in workflows section.
