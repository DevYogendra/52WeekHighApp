## 52-Week High Concepts

### **52-Week High**
- **Definition:** The highest stock price a company has traded at in the last 52 weeks (1 year)
- **Why it matters:** When a stock reaches a new 52W high, it signals momentum and investor conviction
- **Interpretation:** Stocks at 52W highs are typically:
  - Outperformers in their sector
  - Found by smart money first
  - Likely to continue if fundamentals support
- **In the app:** Primary filter across all views

### **Within 5% of 52W High**
- **Definition:** Stock price is within 5% below the highest price of the year
- **Example:** If 52W high is ₹100, this range is ₹95-₹100
- **Interpretation:** Stock is at peak momentum levels
- **Trading implication:** May continue higher OR could start correcting
- **View:** "Within 5% of 52W High"

### **Percentage Down from 52W High (↓52W High%)**
- **Definition:** How far the current price is below the 52W high
- **Formula:** (52W High - Current Price) / 52W High × 100
- **Examples:**
  - 0-5% = Near peak (Within 5% view)
  - 5-50% = Moderate pullback (5-50% view)
  - 50%+ = Severe discount (Big Dippers view)
- **Use case:** Segment stocks by how much they've fallen

## Valuation Metrics

### **Price-to-Earnings Ratio (P/E)**
- **Definition:** Stock Price ÷ Earnings Per Share (EPS)
- **What it measures:** How many years of earnings you're paying for the stock
- **Formula:** Market Cap ÷ Net Profit
- **Interpretation:**
  - **P/E < 10:** Very cheap (but check why so cheap)
  - **P/E 10-15:** Undervalued
  - **P/E 15-25:** Fair value
  - **P/E > 25:** Premium/expensive
- **Important:** Compare to:
  - Company's own history
  - Industry average
  - Growth rate (see PEG)
- **Pro tip:** Low P/E + high growth = Best opportunity

### **Price-to-Book Value Ratio (P/BV)**
- **Definition:** Stock Price ÷ Book Value Per Share (Assets - Liabilities ÷ Shares)
- **What it measures:** How much premium you're paying vs asset value
- **Interpretation:**
  - **P/BV < 1:** Trading below book value (potential value)
  - **P/BV 1-3:** Fair to reasonable
  - **P/BV 3-7:** Premium (usually growth/quality)
  - **P/BV > 7:** Very expensive
- **Best for:** Asset-heavy industries
  - Banks and Financials: High P/BV normal
  - Real Estate: Compare to NAV
  - Software/IT: Less relevant (low assets)
- **Warning:** Very low P/BV often indicates trouble (why so cheap?)

### **PEG Ratio (Price-Earnings-Growth)**
- **Definition:** P/E Ratio ÷ Earnings Growth Rate (%)
- **Formula:** P/E ÷ 3-Year EPS CAGR
- **What it measures:** Whether valuation is justified by growth
- **Interpretation:**
  - **PEG < 1.0:** Undervalued relative to growth (BUY sweet spot)
  - **PEG 1.0-2.0:** Fair valuation
  - **PEG > 2.0:** Expensive relative to growth rate
- **Example:**
  - Stock: P/E 30, Growth 50% → PEG = 0.6 (Great!)
  - Stock: P/E 30, Growth 10% → PEG = 3.0 (Overpriced)
- **Pro tip:** **Most reliable metric for growth stock evaluation**

### **Earnings Yield (E/P)**
- **Definition:** Earnings Per Share ÷ Stock Price (Inverse of P/E)
- **Formula:** Net Profit ÷ Market Cap
- **What it measures:** Annual return from company earnings
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Yield > 10%:** Good return from earnings
  - **Yield 5-10%:** Decent
  - **Yield < 5%:** Low return from earnings
- **Compare to:** Interest rates
  - If bank FD = 6%, stock yield 10% = good value
  - If bank FD = 6%, stock yield 3% = need growth
- **Use case:** Bond-like comparison approach

## Quality/Efficiency Metrics

### **Return on Equity (ROE)**
- **Definition:** Net Profit ÷ Shareholder Equity
- **What it measures:** How efficiently the company generates profits from shareholder money
- **Formula:** Net Profit ÷ Average Shareholder Equity
- **Interpretation:**
  - **ROE > 20%:** Excellent (Top tier)
  - **ROE 15-20%:** Very good
  - **ROE 10-15%:** Good
  - **ROE 5-10%:** Acceptable
  - **ROE < 5%:** Poor
- **Industry context:**
  - Banks: 12-18% is normal
  - IT Services: 20-30% is normal
  - Real Estate: 8-12% is normal
- **Red flag:** High ROE + Low P/E + 52W high = Hidden gem
- **Question to ask:** How sustainable is this ROE?

### **Return on Assets (ROA)**
- **Definition:** Net Profit ÷ Total Assets
- **What it measures:** How efficiently using ALL capital (equity + debt)
- **Interpretation:**
  - **ROA > 10%:** Excellent
  - **ROA 5-10%:** Good
  - **ROA 2-5%:** Moderate
  - **ROA < 2%:** Poor
- **Compare ROE vs ROA:**
  - If ROE 20% but ROA 5% → Using debt heavily (risky if rates rise)
  - If ROE 15% and ROA 12% → Conservative, stable

### **Operating Profit Margin (OPM) & OPM Last Year**
- **Definition:** Operating Profit ÷ Sales (Revenue)
- **What it measures:** Profitability from core business operations
- **Formula:** (Revenue - Operating Expenses) ÷ Revenue
- **Interpretation:**
  - **OPM > 20%:** Highly profitable operations
  - **OPM 15-20%:** Good profitability
  - **OPM 10-15%:** Moderate
  - **OPM 5-10%:** Low margins, competitive industry
  - **OPM < 5%:** Very thin margins
- **Trend matters:** Compare to Last Year
  - Rising OPM = Improving efficiency ✅
  - Stable OPM = Consistent
  - Falling OPM = Margin pressure ⚠️
- **Industry context:** Varies hugely
  - Software: 20-40% normal
  - Retail: 3-5% normal
  - Banks: 40-50% normal

## Momentum & Flow Metrics

### **Delta % Market Cap Gain (Δ% MCap)**
- **Definition:** % change in market cap since stock first hit 52W high
- **Formula:** (Current MCap - First MCap) / First MCap × 100
- **What it measures:** Market cap appreciation since stock entered 52W high list
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Positive gain:** Market grew confidence in stock ✅
  - **High gain (>50%):** Strong fundamental improvement
  - **Modest gain (5-20%):** Normal, recent entry
  - **Flat (0-5%):** Likely recent entry into highs
- **Quality signal:** Positive gain + Repeated highs = Quality momentum
- **Time since appearance:** Newer entries wont have big gains yet

### **Δ Hits (Delta Hits - Week-over-Week)**
- **Definition:** Difference in number of 52W high appearances between this week and last week
- **Meaning:** Is the stock getting MORE or FEWER highs?
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Δ Hits > 0:** More high appearances this week (Accelerating) ⬆️
  - **Δ Hits = 0:** Same frequency (Steady)
  - **Δ Hits < 0:** Fewer appearances this week (Decelerating) ⬇️
- **Use:** Spot early trend changes
- **In view:** "Trend Shift Analyzer"

### **Δ Gain% (Delta Gain - Week-over-Week)**
- **Definition:** Difference in market cap gains between this week and last week
- **Formula:** (Gain% This Week) - (Gain% Last Week)
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Positive:** Stock growing faster this week (Acceleration) ⬆️
  - **Negative:** Growth slowing from last week (Deceleration) ⬇️
- **Use:** Confirm momentum changes with market cap growth

### **Δ DII Holding (Change in Domestic Institutional Investor Holdings)**
- **Definition:** % point change in DII ownership in the stock
- **Meaning:** Are domestic institutions (banks, mutual funds, insurance) buying or selling?
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Positive change:** Institutions accumulating (Bullish) ✅
  - **Negative change:** Institutions selling (Bearish) ⚠️
  - **Size matters:** +2% is significant, +0.1% is minor
- **Why it matters:** Big institutions do research, their moves matter
- **Examples:**
  - +2.5%: Major buying by institutions
  - -1.5%: Institutions rotating out

### **Δ FII Holding (Change in Foreign Institutional Investor Holdings)**
- **Definition:** % point change in FII ownership
- **Meaning:** Are foreign investors (Goldman, BlackRock, etc.) buying or selling?
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Positive:** Foreign money flowing in (Bullish) ✅
  - **Negative:** Foreign capitulation (Can be bearish or capitulation opportunity)
- **Macro context:** FII flows also depend on rupee, global rates
- **Insight:** FII > DII typically means foreign confidence

## Balance Sheet Health

### **Debt-to-Equity Ratio (D/E)**
- **Definition:** Total Debt ÷ Total Equity
- **What it measures:** Financial leverage - how much debt company uses vs owner capital
- **Interpretation:**
  - **D/E < 0.5:** Very conservative (low risk) ✅
  - **D/E 0.5-1.0:** Moderate, healthy
  - **D/E 1.0-1.5:** Moderate-high, acceptable
  - **D/E 1.5-2.0:** High leverage (risky)
  - **D/E > 2.0:** Very high risk ❌
- **Exception:** Some industries have high D/E by nature
  - Banks: D/E 5-10 is normal
  - Real Estate: D/E 1.5-3 is normal
- **Risk factor:** High D/E + falling earnings = Risk of distress

### **Cash Position & Liquidity**
- **Definition:** Cash on hand ÷ Current liabilities
- **What it measures:** Can company pay short-term obligations?
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Ratio > 1.0:** Strong liquidity
  - **Ratio 0.5-1.0:** Normal
  - **Ratio < 0.5:** May have cash crunch

## Cash Flow & Working Capital

### **Trade Receivables**
- **Definition:** Money owed TO the company by customers
- **What it measures:** How much uncollected sales exist
- **Red flag:** Receivables growing much faster than sales
  - Example: Sales +10%, Receivables +50% = Collection risk ⚠️
- **Good sign:** Receivables declining while sales growing
  - Better cash collection

### **Trade Payables**
- **Definition:** Money the company OWES to suppliers
- **What it measures:** How long company takes to pay suppliers
- **Good sign:** Payables growing = Company negotiating better terms
- **Balance sheet flow:** Often a source of working capital funding

### **Inventory**
- **Definition:** Stock of goods not yet sold
- **Metrics:**
  - **Inventory Turnover:** Sales ÷ Average Inventory
    - Higher = Better (Faster selling, better cash)
    - Compare to prior years
- **Red flags:**
  - Rising inventory while sales flat/declining = Slowdown
  - Inventory write-offs = Quality issues
- **Good sign:** Inventory declining while sales maintained

### **Working Capital = Receivables + Inventory - Payables**
- **What it measures:** Cash tied up in operations
- **Rising working capital needs:** More cash required to run business
- **Declining working capital:** Better cash conversion

## Trend Score Concepts

### **Trend Score (Used in Trend Analyzer)**
- **Definition:** Weighted count of 52W high appearances across non-overlapping windows
- **Formula:**
  ```
  Trend Score = (Hits in 0-7 days × 3) +
                (Hits in 8-30 days × 2) +
                (Hits in 31-60 days × 1)
  ```
- **Why weighting?** Recent performance matters more
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Score > 30:** Exceptional (Hottest stocks)
  - **Score 20-30:** Very good
  - **Score 10-20:** Good
  - **Score 5-10:** Moderate
  - **Score < 5:** Weak

### **Acceleration (Used in Trend Analyzer)**
- **Definition:** How fast hit rate is changing
- **Formula:**
  ```
  Acceleration = (Daily rate in recent window) - (Daily rate in prior window)
                = (Hits 0-7 ÷ 7) - (Hits 8-30 ÷ 23)
  ```
- **Positive:** Getting more hits per day (Accelerating) ⬆️
- **Negative:** Getting fewer hits per day (Decelerating) ⬇️
- **Zero:** Same rate (Steady)
- **Use:** Spot inflection points early

### **Persistence Score (Used in Multi-Bagger Hunt)**
- **Definition:** Composite score of consistency + acceleration + market cap growth
- **Formula:**
  ```
  Score = 40% × (Recent/Medium Acceleration) +
          30% × (Medium/Older Sustenance) +
          30% × (Market Cap Gain %)
  ```
- **Interpretation:**
  - **Score > 50:** Excellent persistence (Likely multi-bagger)
  - **Score 20-50:** Good persistence
  - **Score < 20:** Weak or inconsistent
- **Use:** Find stocks likely to deliver 3-5x+ returns

## Investment Strategies

### **Momentum Investing**
- **Core idea:** Stocks moving higher tend to continue
- **Buy signal:** Stock near 52W high + positive acceleration
- **Sell signal:** Trend breaks (P&L goes negative)
- **Time horizon:** 1-3 months typically
- **Views to use:** "Within 5% of 52W High", "Trend Shift Analyzer"

### **Quality Growth**
- **Core idea:** Buy good companies at reasonable prices
- **Buy signal:** High ROE + Consistent highs + Reasonable P/E
- **Sell signal:** Valuation becomes excessive (P/E > 30)
- **Time horizon:** 6-24 months
- **Views to use:** "Trend Analyzer", "Momentum Summary"

### **Value Investing**
- **Core idea:** Buy beaten-down quality cheap
- **Buy signal:** 20-50% pullback + fundamentals intact + positive signals returning
- **Sell signal:** Stock recovers to fair valuation
- **Time horizon:** 1-3 years
- **Views to use:** "5-50% from 52W High", "Trend Shift Analyzer"

### **Value + Momentum Hybrid**
- **Core idea:** Buy value but wait for momentum confirmation
- **Buy signal:** Discounted + Starting to show highs again (emerging winner)
- **Sell signal:** Momentum breaks
- **Time horizon:** 3-12 months
- **Views to use:** "5-50% from 52W High" + "Emerging Winners"

### **Deep Value / Turnarounds (High Risk)**
- **Core idea:** Buy severely depressed + catalyst + management change
- **Buy signal:** 50%+ down + Clear catalyst + Solid fundamentals remaining
- **Sell signal:** Catalyst doesn't work OR fundamentals deteriorate
- **Time horizon:** 2-5 years
- **Views to use:** "Big Dippers" + Heavy research required

## Words You'll See

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Capitalization / Market Cap** | Total value of company (Stock Price × Shares Outstanding) |
| **Valuation** | How expensive or cheap a stock is |
| **Momentum** | Direction and speed of price movement |
| **Trend** | Direction over longer period |
| **Rotation** | Money moving from one sector/stock to another |
| **Headwinds** | Negative pressures (industry challenges, competition) |
| **Tailwinds** | Positive helps (industry growth, new markets, regulations) |
| **Catalyst** | Event that could trigger stock move (earnings, product, management) |
| **Pullback** | Temporary correction/dip in price |
| **Correction** | Larger decline (10-30%) from recent high |
| **Crash** | Severe decline (30%+) |
| **Rebound** | Recovery from decline |
| **Breakout** | Stock breaking above key resistance |
| **Breakeven** | Price where gain/loss is zero |
| **Fundamentals** | Business basics (earnings, growth, margins) |
| **Technicals** | Price/volume patterns |
| **Due Diligence** | Research before investing |
