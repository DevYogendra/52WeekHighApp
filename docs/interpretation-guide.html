The Big Picture: Why 52-Week Highs Work

Three Core Principles

1 Momentum is Real: Stocks hitting new 52-week highs have the strongest recent earnings surprises, insider buying, institutional accumulation, and positive sentiment. They're fundamentally stronger than the broader market.
2 Trends Persist (Short-Term): A stock that just hit a new high is likely to show continued strength for 3-12 weeks as the trend plays out. The forces that got it there (earnings growth, industry tailwinds, etc.) don't disappear overnight.
3 Quality Compounds: The best new highs come from companies with high ROE, low debt, and growing earnings. These compounds over years. A new high + good quality scores = best risk/reward profile for long-term wealth building.

Why this matters for YOU: Instead of analyzing 5,000 stocks, you focus on the ~50-100 hitting new highs this week. Pre-filtered by momentum. Then add quality checks. This is how you find 10-baggers before they become famous.

Historical Truth: Most +5x gains came from stocks that started by hitting new 52-week highs. This is not theory; it's pattern recognition from thousands of trades. When new highs + quality metrics align, wealth is created.

Essential Columns to Understand First

These 8 columns make 80% of your investment decision. Master these first:

💰 Price

Current stock trading price.

Use: Affects position sizing and psychological comfort. A ₹20 stock vs ₹500 stock doesn't change the analysis, but position sizing does (you can buy 50 shares vs 2 shares for same capital).

✓ Good: Any price point. Pick position size accordingly.

✗ Bad: None inherently. But very high prices (>₹10,000) are rare and often mature companies.

📊 % from 52W High

How close the stock is to its 52-week peak, expressed as percentage discount.

% from High = (Current Price - 52W High) / 52W High × 100

Interpretation:

  • -0% to -3%: At peak, maximum strength, but also highest risk of pullback
  • -3% to -10%: Near high, still strong momentum, safer entry than at peak
  • -10% to -30%: Pullback zone, balanced entry, good for quality-focused buyers
  • -30% to -50%: Deep pullback, good for value hunters, needs careful quality check
  • -50%+: Very deep discount, either recovery play or company in trouble (RESEARCH THOROUGHLY)
📈 Trend Score

Composite momentum indicator combining hit frequency at different time horizons.

Trend Score = (Hits past 7d × 3) + (Hits past 30d × 2) + (Hits past 60d × 1)

Interpretation:

  • 100+: Strong momentum, stock is on fire, highest visibility
  • 60-100: Solid momentum, established trend, safe entry
  • 30-60: Moderate momentum, emerging trend, building
  • <30: Weak momentum, trending but not strong
📉 Δ Hits (Delta Hits)

Change in number of new 52-week highs from one week to the next. Shows if momentum is accelerating or decelerating.

Δ Hits = Hits this week - Hits last week

Interpretation:

  • +3 or higher: Accelerating momentum, STRONG BUY SIGNAL
  • +1 to +3: Growing momentum, positive but not explosive
  • 0 to -1: Stable or slightly declining, still okay but watch carefully
  • -2 or lower: Momentum collapsing, SELL SIGNAL or stay out
💚 % Gain from Low

Percentage gain from the 52-week low. Shows total upside captured so far.

% Gain = (Current Price - 52W Low) / 52W Low × 100

Interpretation:

  • 0-50%: Early stage move, plenty of runway ahead
  • 50-150%: Middle stage move, still healthy, established trend
  • 150-300%: Late stage move, large move already captured, pullback risk
  • 300%+: Very extended, likely overextended, high pullback risk
📊 Δ Gain % (Delta Gain)

Change in gain percentage from one week to previous week. Shows if the stock is accelerating or decelerating in gains.

Interpretation:

  • Positive (higher each week): Acceleration, momentum building, STRONG
  • Flat (similar week-to-week): Momentum stable, healthy
  • Negative (declining week-to-week): Deceleration, momentum fading, CAUTION
⏰ Days to Hit High

How many days since the stock last hit a 52-week high. Shows recent activity.

Interpretation:

  • 0-7 days: Very recently hit high, momentum very fresh, maximum strength
  • 7-30 days: Hit high recently, momentum established but cooling
  • 30+ days: Hit high a while ago, trend might be aging
📊 Frequency Timeline (7D, 30D, 60D)

Number of times stock hit 52-week high in 7-day, 30-day, and 60-day windows.

Interpretation:

  • Increasing trend (7D < 30D < 60D): Momentum building over 2 months, STRONG
  • Flat (similar across all windows): Consistent hitter, reliable but familiar
  • Decreasing trend (7D > 30D > 60D): Momentum declining, been hotter in past, might be peak

Valuation Columns Deep Dive

How expensive is the stock relative to its earnings? These three work together:

💵 P/E Ratio

Price-to-Earnings. How many years of current earnings you're paying for the stock.

P/E = Stock Price / Earnings Per Share

Example: Stock price ₹100, EPS ₹5 → P/E = 20. You're paying 20 years of earnings.

Interpretation:

  • 5-10: Very cheap, often old economy companies or recovery plays
  • 10-15: Cheap to fair value, quality companies trading at discounts
  • 15-25: Fair to slightly expensive, typical for growing companies
  • 25-40: Expensive, market expects significant growth
  • 40+: Very expensive, a lot of hope priced in, higher risk
  • <2: Either broken or too cheap to be true, RESEARCH

Remember: Lower P/E isn't always better. A company with P/E 8 and declining earnings is risky. P/E 25 with 40% earnings growth is safe.

📚 P/BV (Price-to-Book Value)

Price relative to book value (assets - liabilities). How much you're paying per rupee of asset backing.

P/BV = Market Price / Book Value Per Share

Example: A bank with plenty of assets might have P/BV 1.2. A tech company with few assets might have P/BV 10.

Interpretation:

  • <1.0: Cheap to book value, often distressed or cyclical low
  • 1.0-2.0: Fair value, typical for stable companies
  • 2.0-4.0: Premium to book, usually for quality companies
  • 4.0+: Very expensive to book, high growth expectations built in
📈 PEG Ratio

Price/Earnings-to-Growth. P/E adjusted for growth. The fairness metric.

PEG = P/E Ratio / Expected Annual Earnings Growth Rate (%)

Example: P/E 30, expected growth 30% → PEG = 1.0 (fairly valued for growth). P/E 30, expected growth 10% → PEG = 3.0 (expensive for growth).

Interpretation:

  • <1.0: Cheap relative to growth, excellent value
  • 1.0-1.5: Fair value for growth, sweet spot
  • 1.5-2.0: Slightly expensive for growth
  • 2.0+: Expensive relative to growth, risky
📊 Earnings Yield

Inverse of P/E. What % annual return you'd get if all earnings went to you.

Earnings Yield = 1 / P/E × 100%

Example: P/E 20 → Earnings Yield 5%. Compare this to FD rate (6-7%) for context.

Interpretation:

  • 8%+: High earnings yield, very attractive, cheap
  • 5-8%: Good earnings yield, fair value
  • 3-5%: Low earnings yield, expensive
  • <3%: Very low earnings yield, risky unless super growth story

Quality Metrics Deep Dive

How good is the business? These metrics show profitability and efficiency:

💪 ROE % (Return on Equity)

Profit generated per rupee of shareholder equity. THE most important quality metric.

ROE = Net Income / Shareholders Equity × 100%

Interpretation:

  • 20%+: Exceptional quality, best-in-class, exceptional businesses
  • 15-20%: Very good quality, excellent companies
  • 12-15%: Good quality, solid companies
  • 8-12%: Average quality, acceptable but not special
  • <8%: Poor quality, company barely making returns beyond risk-free rate
  • <0%: Losing money, stay away

Pro Tip: ROE 15%+ combined with new 52W high is your holy grail signal. High ROE + momentum = wealth building.

📊 ROA % (Return on Assets)

Profit per rupee of total assets. Shows how efficiently company uses everything it owns.

ROA = Net Income / Total Assets × 100%

Interpretation:

  • 10%+: Excellent asset efficiency
  • 5-10%: Good asset efficiency
  • 2-5%: Average asset efficiency
  • <2%: Poor asset efficiency
💰 OPM % (Operating Profit Margin)

What profit % the company makes from operations, before interest/taxes. Operating efficiency.

OPM = Operating Profit / Sales × 100%

Interpretation (varies by industry):

  • 15%+: Excellent, high-quality business, pricing power
  • 10-15%: Good profitability
  • 5-10%: Average profitability
  • <5%: Low profitability, risky, competitive
  • <0%: Loss-making operations, stay away
📈 Net Profit Margin

Final profit after all expenses, as % of sales.

NPM = Net Income / Sales × 100%

Interpretation:

  • 15%+: Exceptional profitability
  • 10-15%: Very good profitability
  • 5-10%: Good profitability
  • 3-5%: Average profitability
  • <3%: Low profitability

Momentum Indicators Deep Dive

📈 Market Cap Change %

Δ % MCap. Week-over-week change in market capitalization. Shows if money is flowing in.

Interpretation:

  • +5%+: Strong money inflow, BULLISH
  • +2% to +5%: Healthy money inflow, positive
  • -2% to +2%: Stable, no strong direction
  • -5%+: Money outflow, BEARISH
💳 DII/FII Flows

Domestic Institutional Investor and Foreign Institutional Investor net buying/selling.

Interpretation:

  • Both positive: Smart money (institutions) buying, very bullish
  • DII positive, FII neutral: Domestic buying, positive for India-focused investors
  • One positive, one negative: Mixed signals, unclear
  • Both negative: Institutions selling heavily, very bearish
📊 Volatility

Standard deviation of returns. High volatility = more price swings.

Interpretation:

  • <20% annual: Low volatility, slower but steadier movements, safer
  • 20-35% annual: Moderate volatility, typical for Indian stocks
  • 35%+: High volatility, wild swings, higher risk of sudden stops

Balance Sheet Health

⚖️ Debt/Equity Ratio

Total Debt ÷ Total Equity. Shows how much the company is leveraged.

Example: D/E 0.5 means ₹1 of debt for every ₹2 of equity. Safe. D/E 2.0 means ₹2 of debt for every ₹1 of equity. Risky.

Interpretation:

  • <0.3: Very low debt, strong financial position, very safe
  • 0.3-0.7: Low debt, healthy, good financially
  • 0.7-1.0: Moderate debt, acceptable for growing companies
  • 1.0-1.5: High debt, risky in downturns
  • 1.5-2.0: Very high debt, risky, scrutinize carefully
  • 2.0+: Extremely high debt, high distress risk

Always Check: Always verify D/E < 1.0 before buying. It takes 5 seconds and saves you from leverage disasters.

💧 Current Ratio

Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities. Can the company pay short-term bills?

Interpretation:

  • 1.5+: Strong liquidity, can pay all short-term debts easily
  • 1.0-1.5: Adequate liquidity
  • <1.0: Concerning, might struggle to pay bills
📦 Inventory Turnover

How many times per year the company sells and replaces inventory.

Interpretation:

  • Higher is better: Company sells inventory quickly, cash flow efficient
  • Very low: Inventory sits unsold, cash locked up, possibly obsolete

All 11 Views Explained

1 Start Here Dashboard
Purpose: Quick snapshot of all metrics across 11 categories in one dashboard

Best for: Orientation. See the overall health of new highs in one place.

Key columns: All broad metrics (P/E, ROE, momentum, etc.)

Action: Review weekly to understand market tone. Then dive into specific views based on what you see.

2 Trend Analyzer (Consistent Performers)
Purpose: Stocks hitting new highs consistently over months with stable high Trend Scores

Best for: Quality growth investors and medium-term holders (3-12 months)

Ranking: By Trend Score (hits × 3 + hits × 2 + hits × 1) and Acceleration

Key signal: High Trend Score + positive Δ Hits = strong continuation likely

Action: Buy quality stocks from this view for 3-12 month holding period. Focus on ROE 15%+, D/E <0.7.

3 Trend Shift (Week-over-Week Momentum Changes)
Purpose: Stocks with changing momentum: accelerating highs or decelerating trends

Best for: Swing traders and momentum chasers (2-8 weeks)

Key metrics: Δ Hits and Δ Gain %

Green flag: Both Δ Hits and Δ Gain % are positive = acceleration

Red flag: Both negative = deceleration, momentum dying

Action: Buy green flags early (Trend Score 40-60), exit red flags quickly. Trade 2-8 week cycles.

4 Emerging Winners (Recently Accelerating)
Purpose: Stocks recently hitting new highs with rising momentum and market cap

Best for: Early-stage momentum traders (4-12 weeks)

Key signal: Recent high + accelerating Δ Hits + rising market cap

Sweet spot: "Days to High" = 0-14 days AND Δ Hits positive AND gaining volume

Action: First movers get best returns. Buy these early, exit when Δ becomes negative.

5 Momentum Summary (Ranked by Hit Frequency)
Purpose: All new high hitters ranked by 7D, 30D, 60D frequency. Shows consistency.

Best for: Balanced investors wanting both quality and momentum

Key insight: If 7D < 30D < 60D (increasing) = momentum building over 2 months = strong

Key insight: If 7D > 30D > 60D (decreasing) = momentum recently peaked = be cautious

Action: Focus on stocks with increasing frequency. These show accelerating trends.

6 Multi-Bagger Hunt (Persistence Scoring)
Purpose: Stocks scored for likelihood of 5-10x returns based on persistence and volatility

Best for: Long-term wealth builders (1-3+ years)

Scoring: 40% frequency score (showing persistence) + 30% historical gain + 30% recent performance

Key signal: High persistence score + high frequency 30-60d = likely multi-baggers

Action: Pick top 10 from this view, buy small positions (0.5% portfolio each), hold 2-3 years. Some 5-10x.

7 Within 5% of 52W High (Strongest Movers)
Purpose: Only stocks at or within 5% of their 52-week high. Maximum strength zone.

Best for: Quality growth and breakout buyers

Key insight: These are the TRUE leaders. Fought through resistance, winning.

Red flag: If in this view + P/E >40 + Δ Hits negative = overbought, skip

Green flag: If in this view + ROE 15%+ + D/E <0.7 + Δ Hits positive = buy signal

Action: Screen for quality stocks in this view. Buy the best, hold 6-24 months.

8 Pullback Candidates (5-50% Below High)
Purpose: Quality companies that recently pulled back 5-50% from highs. Buying opportunities.

Best for: Value investors and quality hunters

Strategy: Find quality companies (ROE 12%+, D/E <0.7) that hit highs recently but pulled back.

Entry logic: Quality + pullback = safer entry to quality stocks with reduced risk

Action: Buy these when -20% to -30% pullback + all fundamentals strong. Wait for bounce back.

9 Big Dippers (50%+ Below High - Deep Value)
Purpose: Stocks trading 50%+ below 52-week high. Deep discounts, recovery or distress plays.

Best for: Deep value investors and contrarians (HIGH RISK/HIGH REWARD)

Critical filter: MUST check why down 50%. Company in trouble? Market panic? Sector rotation?

Green flag: Good ROE (8%+) + reasonable D/E (<1.5) + recent positive news

Red flag: Negative ROE or D/E >2.0 = distressed, too risky

Action: Research thoroughly before buying. If thesis is sound, buy small (0.2-0.5%), scale in on further lows.

10 Industry Tailwinds (Sector Analysis)
Purpose: Shows which sectors have most new highs, most momentum. Sector trends.

Best for: Sector rotators and macro-aware investors

Key insight: 20 new highs in IT vs 2 in Auto = IT hot, Auto cold. Follow the hot sectors.

Action: First filter: Which sectors have most new highs? Then go to View 2/4 and pick best stocks IN those sectors.

Why it works: Sector momentum + individual momentum = compounding winners.

Best for: Navigation and orientation

5 Complete Investment Workflows

🚀 Workflow 1: Momentum Acceleration (2-8 Week Swing Trades)

Goal: Catch recent acceleration in early stages before mainstream awareness

Who it's for: Active traders comfortable with 2-8 week positions

Step 1
Go to View 3 (Trend Shift)
Step 2
Filter: Δ Hits >1 AND Δ Gain % positive
Step 3
Sort by Trend Score 40-60 (not overbought)

Entry: Buy when Trend Score enters 40-60 zone + Δ Hits positive

Exit: When Δ Hits turns negative OR Trend Score >100 (overbought)

Position Size: 1-2% per trade (higher risk)

Risk: Can reverse fast. Use stop losses at -10%.

📈 Workflow 2: Quality Growth (6-24 Month Buy & Hold)

Goal: Build wealth slowly by holding quality companies hitting new highs

Who it's for: Patient investors with 6+ month time horizons

Step 1
View 2 (Trend Analyzer)
Step 2
Sort by ROE (high to low)
Step 3
Filter: ROE 15%+, D/E <0.7, P/E 12-25

Entry: Buy when all filters are green. Dollar cost average over 4-6 weeks.

Hold: Don't sell until 6-24 months have passed or ROE drops 5%+

Position Size: 2-3% per stock (lower risk)

Advantage: Quality compounds. Most 10-baggers come from 3-5 year holds of 15%+ ROE companies.

💎 Workflow 3: Value + Momentum Hybrid (3-12 Month Holds)

Goal: Find companies with good fundamentals that are accelerating. Best of both worlds.

Who it's for: Balanced investors wanting quality + momentum

Step 1
View 8 (Pullback Candidates)
Step 2
Filter: ROE 12%+, D/E <0.8
Step 3
Find those showing positive Δ Hits (momentum returning)

Entry: Buy when pullback stock starts showing positive momentum again. Double winner signal.

Logic: Good company + value discount + momentum returning = very strong setup

Position Size: 2-3% per stock (medium risk)

Best Risk/Reward: Combines worst-case downside protection (quality + discount) with upside (momentum)

🔥 Workflow 4: Deep Value Contrarian (2-5 Year Holds)

Goal: Find severely beaten-down companies that will bounce back. Highest risk/reward.

Who it's for: Research-heavy investors comfortable with 2-5 year waits

Step 1
View 9 (Big Dippers)
Step 2
Filter: 50-70% below high (worst hit, best recovery potential)
Step 3
Check ROE 8%+ and D/E <1.5 (not dead companies)

Research step: For each candidate, research: Why did it crash? Is it permanent or temporary? Any turnaround signals?

Entry: ONLY buy if you have thesis confirmed. Buy 0.2-0.5% position, then scale in on every 10% further drop.

Exit: Take 25-50% profits on first bounce (fast money), then hold remaining for 3+ years

Warning: Can go to zero. Never more than 1% of portfolio in any one deep value bet.

🌍 Workflow 5: Sector Rotation (Tactical, Every 4 Weeks)

Goal: Follow sector momentum. Rotate capital into hot sectors.

Who it's for: Macro-aware investors who like adapting to market cycles

Step 1
View 10 (Industry Tailwinds)
Step 2
Count new highs per sector. Which sectors have 10+?
Step 3
Go to View 4 (Emerging Winners), filter by hot sector

Action: Build 30-50% portfolio in hot sector stocks, rebalance every 4 weeks.

Why it works: Sector momentum is real. IT in bull phase → 40% of portfolio in INFY/TCS/WIPRO. But rotate when sector cools.

Position Size: 2-5% per stock, 30-50% per sector (medium risk)

Note: Requires discipline to rotate regularly. But outperforms buy-and-hold in cyclic markets.
