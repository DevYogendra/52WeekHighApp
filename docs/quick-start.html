Your 30-Second Overview

What is this app? It finds Indian stocks hitting 52-week highs and shows them through 11 different analytical lenses (momentum, value, quality, etc.).
Why use 52-week highs? Stocks hitting new highs often have the strongest fundamentals, momentum, and likelihood of continued growth.
The decision framework: (1) Pick the view that matches your investment style (2) Check financial metrics (3) Execute your workflow

Your 5-Minute Walking Tour

Here's exactly what to do:

⏱️ Step 1: Start at View 1 (Start Here Dashboard)

You'll see 11 quick metrics across momentum, quality, and value - this is your orientation point.

⏱️ Step 2: Find Which View Calls to You

Skim Views 2-11 in the sidebar. Read their descriptions (shown when you click them) and see which one aligns with your thinking.

⏱️ Step 3: Click Into That View

You'll see an interactive table with 20-30 columns. Don't panic - only 5-8 columns matter for your decision (see the table below).

⏱️ Step 4: Sort and Filter

Click the column headers to sort. Look for patterns: Good P/E? Healthy debt? Strong ROE? These are your checkboxes.

⏱️ Step 5: Deep Dive into Your Top 3 Picks

For each stock you like, check: P/E ratio (value), ROE (quality), Debt/Equity (safety), and momentum trends. See "Red Flags" below.

⏱️ Step 6: Execute Your Decision

Use the workflows below that match your style. Buy, wait for pullback, or skip - each view provides a different signal.

📊 Columns You Need to Know First

Don't overwhelm yourself with 30 columns. These 5-8 matter most for your decision:

Column What It Means Good Sign Bad Sign
Price Current stock price Any price point, but affects position sizing Extremely high/low can signal extremes
P/E Ratio Price ÷ Earnings per share 10-20 (medium), <10 (cheap), <5 (very cheap) >30 (expensive), <2 (risky)
ROE % Return on equity (profit ÷ equity) 15%+ (excellent), 12-15% (good) <8% (poor quality)
D/E Ratio Debt ÷ Equity (cash health) <0.5 (strong), <1.0 (healthy) >2.0 (risky), >3.0 (dangerous)
Δ Hits How many more times hit high this week vs last week +2, +3 (accelerating), 0-1 (stable) -2, -3 (decelerating)
52W High % How far from 52-week high right now 0-3% (near high, strong), -20% (pullback opportunity) -60%+ (likely deep value, needs research)
Trend Score Composite momentum indicator 75+ (strong trend) <30 (weak trend)
Pro Tip: Sort by P/E first (to find cheaper stocks) or ROE first (to find quality). Then check D/E to ensure safety. That 3-column check handles 70% of your decision-making.

🔄 Your 3 Practical Workflows

Workflow 1: Find a Quality Stock Hitting New Highs (Long Hold)
Start in View 7 (Within 5% of 52W High) - these are the leaders
Sort by ROE (descending) - find those with ROE > 15%
Check Debt/Equity: Should be < 1.0 (ideally < 0.5)
Check P/E: Should be 12-20 (not too cheap, not expensive)
Look at Δ Hits: Positive is good (momentum still there), declining is warning
Decision: If all green, buy for 1-3 year hold. If any red flag, skip and find next one
Workflow 2: Catch a Stock Before It Rockets (Momentum Play - 3-6 Months)
Start in View 3 (Trend Shift) or View 4 (Emerging Winners) - early momentum signs
Find stocks with accelerating Δ Hits (went from 0 to +2 or higher recently)
Check Gain %: Should be showing positive momentum but not yet sky-high (200%+)
Quick sanity check: Is P/E reasonable (<30) and ROE positive (>5%)?
Entry: Buy early entries when Trend Score is 40-60 (not overextended)
Exit: When Δ Hits goes negative (momentum fading) or at +100% gain
Workflow 3: Find a Deep Value Bargain (Contrarian - 2-5 Year Hold)
Start in View 9 (Deep Dippers) or View 8 (Pullback Candidates) - find the discounts
Filter down to 50%+ discounts (these require serious homework)
Check the company basics: ROE > 8%? Debt/Equity reasonable (< 1.5)?
Ask yourself: Why is it down 50%? (Bad earnings? Market panic? Sector crisis?)
Research: Only if reason for fall seems temporary, not structural
Entry: Buy 25% of intended position, then average down if it drops further (scaling in)
Exit: When it recovers 25-50% from purchase price (take profits) or after 3+ years

🚨 Red Flags & ✅ Green Flags

🚨 Red Flags (SKIP)

P/E < 2 or > 50: Either broken company or speculative bubble. Risky.
ROE < 5%: Company is barely profitable. Low quality.
Debt/Equity > 2: Company is over-leveraged. Risky in downturns.
Δ Hits turning sharply negative: Momentum is dying. Likely peaked.
Operating Margin < 3%: Company barely makes profit on sales. Risky.

✅ Green Flags (BUY)

P/E 12-20 + ROE 15%+: Good valuation + high quality. Sweet spot.
Δ Hits positive & rising: Momentum accelerating. Strong signal.
Debt/Equity < 0.5 + Operating Margin > 10%: Strong fundamentals.
Div Yield > 2% with growing ROE: Making money AND rewarding shareholders.
Hitting new 52W high near end of week: Institutional buying day (higher conviction).

❓ Common Questions & Answers

Q: "Does hitting a 52-week high mean it will keep going up?"

No, it's a prerequisite, not a guarantee. You still need to check quality metrics (ROE, debt), valuation (P/E), and momentum (Δ Hits). A 52-week high + good metrics = likely to continue up.

Q: "Which view should I look at first?"

Start with View 1 (Start Here) for orientation, then pick based on your strategy from the "Investor Styles" section above. Momentum trader → View 2. Value hunter → View 8. Unsure → View 5 (Momentum Summary).

Q: "What does P/E <10 mean? Is that always good?"

P/E <10 usually means cheap. BUT check why (sometimes companies with very low P/E are cheap for a reason - going out of business, high debt, etc.). Always cross-check with ROE and debt/equity.

Q: "I see a stock I like. What's the next step?"

First, read the "Columns You Need to Know First" table above and verify P/E, ROE, D/E all look good. Then decide based on where you found it: New high? → Buy part soon. Pulled back? → Buy if all metrics strong. Crashed? → Buy with small position, scale in.

Q: "How often should I check the app?"

For momentum plays: Every day. High changes → new opportunities daily. For value/long-term: 2x per week is enough (good opportunities don't disappear in 3 days).

Q: "Can I use this for day trading?"

Data updates daily, so this is better for swing trades (3-30 days) and position trading (months-years). Very aggressive day trading is not the app's strength.

Q: "What if I don't understand a column?"

Go to the "Columns to Watch First" table above, or visit the Glossary for detailed explanations of every metric.

Q: "What if I don't see any good opportunities today?"

That's okay. Markets cycle. Don't force trades. Save your capital and wait for the next opportunity (could be tomorrow, could be next week). The best trade is no trade.

💡 Pro Tips to Crush It

Tip 1: Use Multiple Signals

Don't rely on a single metric. If a stock looks great on momentum (Δ Hits), double-check quality (ROE) and valuation (P/E). Single signals are noise; multiple signals are symphony.

Tip 2: Sort the Table by Your Criteria

Click the column headers! Sort by ROE (high to low) to find quality, then look for those near 52W highs. Sort by P/E to find value. This is powerful and takes 3 clicks.

Tip 3: Check the Industry (View 11)

Some sectors are on fire (20 companies hitting highs), others are cold (2 companies). Pick your stock from a hot industry - industry tailwinds are real.

Tip 4: Track Your Picks

Export a few stocks you like from the table. Track them for a week. Did they go up? Why? Did they go down? Why? This builds your intuition faster than anything.

Tip 5: P/E Ratio is Your Starting Point

P/E tells you valuation at a glance. P/E 10 = cheap. P/E 30 = expensive. Most profitable companies cluster around 15-25. Use this as your anchor metric.

Tip 6: 52W High % Shows Momentum Timing

-1% = near peak (very strong momentum, entering now is risky). -20% = pullback (safer entry). -60%+ = deep value (high reward, high risk). Pick your risk/reward ratio.

Tip 7: Don't Overlook Debt/Equity

A cheap stock with high debt can crash in a downturn. Always check D/E (should be <1.0). It takes 5 seconds and saves you 50% portfolio loss.

Tip 8: Combine Views for Powerful Thesis

Find a quality stock (View 2 + high ROE) that's at a pullback (View 8) in a hot industry (View 11). This thesis is bulletproof. Do this weekly and you'll find your best picks.

🔧 Troubleshooting

Q: "The table looks overwhelming. Too many columns."

A: You don't need to understand all 30 columns right now. Focus on the 5-7 "Columns You Need to Know First" (above). Master those, then expand. Quality → Quantity.

Q: "I sorted the table but nothing changed."

A: Click the column header itself (not the data cell). You should see a small arrow appear indicating sort direction. If stuck, refresh the page and try again.

Q: "View 1 shows 11 metrics but I only see 5. Did it break?"

A: Scroll right in the table. The metrics are there, but the screen isn't wide enough to fit all. Mobile users will see this especially.

Q: "Why does View 2 show totally different stocks than View 3?"

A: Each view filters by different criteria. View 2 = consistent performers over time (Trend Score). View 3 = rapid momentum shift (week-over-week). Same data, different lens. Both are valid.

Q: "I don't understand what 'Δ Hits' means."

A: Go to the Glossary and search for "Δ Hits". It explains what it means, why it matters, and how to interpret it. Much more detail there.
