# 📖 Interpretation Guide: How to Read Tables & Understand the Big Picture

## 🎯 The Big Picture: Why 52-Week Highs Matter

### The Core Idea

**52-week highs are a powerful filter for momentum and quality.**

When a stock hits a 52-week high, it means:
- ✓ Investors collectively believe the company is worth this much (or more)
- ✓ The stock is in a confirmed uptrend (not a flash spike that reversed)
- ✓ Multiple periods of strength have pushed it to this level
- ✓ Fundamentals or market sentiment have improved enough to sustain the move

### Investment Philosophy

This application is built on 3 core principles:

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ 1. MOMENTUM WORKS                                            │
│    Stocks moving higher tend to continue moving higher      │
│    (at least in the medium-term). 52W highs capture this.   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 2. LEADERSHIP SHOWS UP                                       │
│    Good companies, good management, good tailwinds show up  │
│    in 52W high lists first. Track where dollars flow.       │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 3. PERSISTENCE BEATS NOISE                                   │
│    A stock hitting 52W high occasionally = noise.           │
│    A stock hitting 52W high repeatedly = signal.            │
│    Use frequency to separate signal from noise.             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 📊 Understanding the Tables: Column Reference

### Essential Columns (Across All Views)

#### **Name** (Company Name)
- **What it is:** Company name, clickable link to Screener.in
- **How to use it:** Click to see full fundamental data, industry, management
- **Example:** Reliance Industries, HDFC Bank, TCS

#### **NSE / BSE** (Stock Exchange Codes)
- **What it is:** National Stock Exchange or Bombay Stock Exchange listing code
- **How to use it:** Use to look up on NSE/BSE websites or other tools
- **Why it matters:** Some tools require the exact code
- **Example:** RELIANCE, HDFCBANK, TCS

#### **MCap** (Market Capitalization)
- **What it is:** Current market cap = Stock Price × Shares Outstanding
- **Measured in:** ₹ (Crores) or ₹ (Billions)
- **How to interpret:**
  - **Large Cap:** MCap > ₹50,000 Cr (Mega cap companies)
  - **Mid Cap:** MCap ₹10,000 - ₹50,000 Cr
  - **Small Cap:** MCap < ₹10,000 Cr
- **Pro tip:** Small caps show bigger % gains; large caps are more stable
- **Example:** Reliance ₹18,00,000 Cr | Bajaj Auto ₹60,000 Cr

#### **First MCap** (Market Cap at First Appearance)
- **What it is:** What was the market cap when this stock first hit 52W high?
- **How to use it:** Compare to current MCap to see growth since entry
- **Example:** Stock first seen at ₹50,000 Cr, now at ₹75,000 Cr
- **Insight:** Growing market cap + repeated highs = Quality momentum

### Valuation Columns (Quality & Price Assessment)

#### **P/E Ratio** (Price-to-Earnings)
- **What it is:** Stock Price ÷ Earnings Per Share
- **How to interpret:**
  - **Low P/E (< 15):** Cheap relative to earnings (Value)
  - **Medium P/E (15-25):** Fair valuation
  - **High P/E (> 25):** Expensive (Growth)
- **Color coding:** Green = Low (Cheap), Yellow = Medium, Red = High (Exp)
- **⚠️ Important:** Low P/E doesn't mean good. Check ROE & growth first.
- **Use case:** 
  - Value hunters: Look for P/E < 20 near highs
  - Growth investors: Look for rising MCap despite rising P/E
- **Example:** TCS P/E 27 (premium quality) vs Small cap P/E 8 (might be value trap)

#### **P/BV Ratio** (Price-to-Book Value)
- **What it is:** Stock Price ÷ Book Value Per Share
- **How to interpret:**
  - **Low P/BV (< 2):** Trading below book value (Good for asset plays)
  - **Medium P/BV (2-5):** Fair
  - **High P/BV (> 5):** Premium (Quality/Growth companies)
- **Best for:** Asset-heavy industries (Financials, Real Estate, Manufacturing)
- **Weak for:** Software, Services (low asset base)
- **Use case:**
  - Value investors: Look for P/BV < 2 with good ROE
  - Quality checklist: P/BV > 3 + P/E > 20 suggests quality premium justified
- **Example:** HDFC Bank P/BV 6 (justified by ROE 15%+) vs Small bank P/BV 0.8 (Why so cheap?)

#### **PEG Ratio** (P/E divided by Growth Rate)
- **What it is:** P/E Ratio ÷ 3-Year Earnings Growth %
- **How to interpret:**
  - **PEG < 1:** Undervalued relative to growth (Good!)
  - **PEG 1-2:** Fair
  - **PEG > 2:** Expensive relative to growth (Be careful)
- **Use case:** **Most reliable for growth stock timing**
- **Example:** Stock with P/E 30 but 50% growth = PEG 0.6 (Good!)

#### **Earnings Yield** (Opposite of P/E)
- **What it is:** Earnings Per Share ÷ Stock Price = E/P (opposite of P/E)
- **How to interpret:**
  - **High Earnings Yield (> 8%):** Good returns from earnings
  - **Compared to:** Interest rates (If rates 6%, yield 8% = attractive)
- **Use case:** Bond-like comparison; higher yield = better value
- **Example:** Yield 10% means:
  - Company earning ₹10 per ₹100 stock price
  - Better than bank FD at 6%

### Quality & Efficiency Columns

#### **ROE** (Return on Equity)
- **What it is:** Profit ÷ Shareholder Equity = How efficiently the company generates profits
- **How to interpret:**
  - **High ROE (> 15%):** Excellent management + competitive advantage
  - **Medium ROE (10-15%):** Good
  - **Low ROE (< 10%):** Mediocre or capital-intensive business
- **🔴 Red flag:** Low ROE at 52W high = Momentum without quality
- **✅ Green flag:** High ROE + Multiple highs = Quality compounding
- **Best companies:** ROE consistently > 15%
- **Example:**
  - TCS ROE 30%+ (Best-in-class)
  - Reliance ROE 8-10% (Capital-intensive but good)
  - Bank ROE 12-20% (Industry dependent)

#### **ROA** (Return on Assets)
- **What it is:** Profit ÷ Total Assets = How efficiently using all capital
- **How to interpret:**
  - **High ROA (> 8%):** Very efficient
  - **Low ROA (< 3%):** Capital-heavy or inefficient
- **Tip:** Compare ROE vs ROA to see if company uses debt heavily
  - If ROE 20% but ROA 8% → Using debt to leverage returns
  - If ROE 12% and ROA 10% → Conservative, stable

#### **OPM** (Operating Profit Margin) & **OPM LY** (Last Year)
- **What it is:** Operating Profit ÷ Sales = Operational efficiency
- **How to interpret:**
  - **High OPM (> 20%):** Very profitable operations
  - **Rising OPM:** Efficiency improving (Good!)
  - **Falling OPM:** Cost pressures or competition (Warning!)
- **Trend is key:** OPM LY tells if margin is improving or declining
- **Example:**
  - Current OPM 22%, Last Year 20% = Improving (Good!)
  - Current OPM 18%, Last Year 22% = Declining (Watch)

### Momentum & Flow Columns

#### **Δ% MCap** (Delta % Market Cap Gain Since First Appearance)
- **What it is:** % Gain in market cap since first hitting 52W high
- **Formula:** (Current MCap - First MCap) / First MCap × 100
- **How to interpret:**
  - **Positive:** Stock appreciated since first 52W high appearance
  - **High gain (> 30%):** Strong fundamental improvement
  - **Low gain (< 5%):** Recent entry into 52W highs
- **Use case:** **Quality check** - Usually correlated with good fundamentals
- **Example:** 
  - +50% means: When first hit 52W high, cap was ₹100 Cr, now ₹150 Cr
  - Indicates market confidence in company

#### **Δ DII Holding / Δ FII Holding** (Foreign & Domestic Institutional Investor Changes)
- **What it is:** % Point change in institutional ownership
- **How to interpret:**
  - **Rising:** Institutions buying (Vote of confidence)
  - **Falling:** Institutions selling (Caution sign)
  - **DIIs = Domestic:** Indian institutions, banks, insurance
  - **FIIs = Foreign:** Foreign investors, PE funds
- **Insight:** Monitoring flow helps spot institutional rotations
- **Example:** +2.5% DII holding = Institutions increased position by 2.5%

#### **↓52W High%** (Percentage Down from 52W High)
- **What it is:** How far the stock has fallen from its 52W high
- **Used in:**
  - **Within 5% of 52W High:** ↓ is 0-5%
  - **5-50% from 52W High:** ↓ is 5-50%
  - **Big Dippers:** ↓ is 50%+
- **Interpretation:**
  - 0-5%: Strong momentum, near peak
  - 20-30%: Healthy pullback, possible re-entry zone
  - 50%+: Severe discount, must understand why

### Balance Sheet & Health Columns

#### **H/E Ratio** (Debt-to-Equity)
- **What it is:** Total Debt ÷ Equity = How much debt company is using
- **How to interpret:**
  - **Low D/E (< 0.5):** Conservative, low financial risk
  - **Medium D/E (0.5-1.5):** Normal, acceptable debt
  - **High D/E (> 2):** Highly leveraged, riskier
- **Industry matters:**
  - Banks: Expected high D/E (normal)
  - Real Estate: Often high D/E (expected)
  - IT Services: Usually low D/E (healthy)
- **⚠️ Warning:** High D/E + falling P/E = Can be risky if earnings disappoint

#### **Trade Receivables / Payables** (Cash Flow Quality)
- **What it is:** Money owed TO company (Receivables) vs Money company OWES (Payables)
- **How to interpret:**
  - **Receivables growing:** Check if customers are paying (Cash flow risk)
  - **Payables growing:** Company paying slower (Better cash management)
  - **Ratio = Receivables / Payables:**
    - Low ratio (< 1): Company stretches payments (Good!)
    - High ratio (> 2): Collecting from customers slowly (May stress)
- **Red flag:** Sales growing but receivables growing much faster = Collection risk

#### **Inventory**
- **What it is:** Stock lying in warehouses, not yet sold
- **How to interpret:**
  - **Rising Inventory:** May indicate slowing sales (Red flag) or building stock
  - **Falling Inventory:** Sales are strong, clearing stock (Good!)
  - **Inventory Turnover = Sales / Average Inventory:**
    - Higher is better (Faster selling)
    - Compare year-over-year

### View 2: **Trend Analyzer** (Non-Overlapping Buckets)
**What it shows:** Stocks ranked by consistency with acceleration metrics

**Key columns:**
| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Hits 0-7D | Times in 52W high in LAST 7 DAYS (current) |
| Hits 8-30D | Times in days 8-30 (prior period, no overlap) |
| Hits 31-60D | Times in days 31-60 (older, no overlap) |
| Trend Score | **Composite:** (0-7D × 3) + (8-30D × 2) + (31-60D × 1) |
| Acceleration | Daily rate change: (0-7D÷7) - (8-30D÷23) |

**How to read:**
- **High Score + Positive Acceleration:** Consistent + Getting Better ✅
- **High Score + Negative Acceleration:** Was good, now cooling ⚠️
- **Low Score + Positive Acceleration:** Just starting up 🚀
- **Low Score + Negative Acceleration:** Fading ❌

**Example:**
```
Stock A: Hits [5, 2, 1] → Score = (5×3)+(2×2)+(1×1) = 22 ✅✅
         Accel = (5÷7) - (2÷23) = +0.624 (Strong acceleration)

Stock B: Hits [1, 4, 3] → Score = (1×3)+(4×2)+(3×1) = 14
         Accel = (1÷7) - (4÷23) = -0.087 (Decelerating)

→ Stock A is better: High score + positive acceleration
```

**What questions it answers:**
- ✅ Which stocks are consistent performers?
- ✅ Which are ACCELERATING?
- ✅ Which are DECELERATING?

**Use case:** Find quality compounders with proven momentum

### View 3: **Trend Shift Analyzer** (Week-over-Week)
**What it shows:** How stocks' momentum changed from last week to this week

**Key columns:**
| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| Hits This | 52W high appearances THIS week |
| Hits Last | 52W high appearances LAST week |
| Δ Hits | This - Last (positive = acceleration) |
| Gain% This | Market cap % gain THIS week |
| Gain% Last | Market cap % gain LAST week |
| Δ Gain% | This - Last (positive = accelerating gains) |

**How to read:**
- **Δ Hits > 0:** More 52W high hits this week (Accelerating) ✅
- **Δ Hits < 0:** Fewer hits this week (Losing steam) ⚠️
- **Δ Gain% > 0:** Market cap growing faster this week (Good!) ✅
- **Δ Gain% < 0:** Market cap growth slowed (Watch) ⚠️

**Best signals:**
- **Both deltas positive:** Clear momentum acceleration ✅✅
- **Hits negative, Gain positive:** Fewer appearances but market cap still growing (Quality?)
- **Both negative:** Losing momentum (Avoid) ❌

**What questions it answers:**
- ✅ Which stocks are accelerating RIGHT NOW?
- ✅ Which are losing momentum?
- ✅ What's the trend direction change?

**Use case:** Weekly portfolio screening, catch trend reversals early

### View 6: **Multi-Bagger Hunt** (Persistence Scoring)
**What it shows:** Stocks ranked by PERSISTENCE (likelihood of being multi-baggers)


**How to read:**
- **Score > 50:** Excellent candidate (High persistence + acceleration)
- **Score 20-50:** Good candidate (Proven consistency)
- **Score < 20:** Weak signal (Inconsistent or new)

**Best signals:**

**What questions it answers:**
- ✅ Which stocks have PROVEN persistence?
- ✅ Which are most likely to become multi-baggers?
- ✅ Which momentum is sustainable, not a flash?

**Use case:** Long-term portfolio building, find future winners early

### View 7: **Within 5% of 52W High** (Breakout Candidates)
**What it shows:** Stocks currently trading NEAR their 52W highs (within 5%)

**↓52W High% should show:** 0-5% (or very small discount)

**Key columns:**
| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ↓52W High% | 0-5% (at the peak) |
| P/E | Price-to-Earnings (Is it expensive?) |
| P/BV | Price-to-Book (Value assessment) |
| ROE | Quality of the business |
| Δ% MCap | Has it grown since first being near highs? |
| Earnings Yield | Alternative way to assess value |

**How to read:**
- **Near high + Rising MCap:** Getting stronger as price rises ✅
- **Near high + High ROE:** Quality near peak (Can continue) ✅
- **Near high + Low P/E:** Cheap despite being near highs (Unusual, check why)
- **Near high + Falling MCap:** Trapped near highs, no growth (Caution) ⚠️
- **Near high + High P/E + No ROE:** Expensive, risky ❌

**Signals to watch:**
- ✅ **High ROE (15%+) + Near high + Rising MCap:** Best case scenario
- ⚠️ **Medium ROE + Near high + Flat MCap:** Sideways trader
- ❌ **Low ROE + Near high + Declining MCap:** Momentum trap

**What questions it answers:**
- ✅ Which stocks have the most momentum?
- ✅ Are they expensive or reasonably valued?
- ✅ Are they quality companies or just noise?
- ✅ Which are breakout candidates vs toppers?

**Use case:** Find quality stocks at confirmation points, momentum trades

- **15-25% pullback:** Often the best risk/reward

**What questions it answers:**
- ✅ Which stocks are offering good entries?
- ✅ Is the pullback a correction or deterioration?
- ✅ Which have the best recovery potential?

### View 9: **Big Dippers (50%+ Down)** (Deep Value)
**What it shows:** Stocks down 50%+ from highs - distressed or deep value

**↓52W High% should show:** 50%+ (Major discount)

**Key columns:**
| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| ↓52W High% | 50%+ discount |
| P/E | Is it cheap? |
| P/BV | Balance sheet value |
| ROE | Real quality or deteriorated? |
| Earnings Yield | Return potential |
| D/E | Debt burden (High D/E + 50% down = risky) |
| Trade Receivables | Customer payment quality |

**How to read (This is CRITICAL):**
- **50% down + P/E < 10 + High ROE + Low D/E:** Classic value trap turnaround ✅
- **50% down + P/E < 8 + ROE < 5% + High D/E:** Value trap, avoid ❌
- **50% down + P/E still high (15+):** Market fears aren't priced in yet ⚠️
- **50% down + P/BV < 1 + High ROE:** Asset-backed value, good hunt ✅

**Questions BEFORE buying:**
1. **Why is it down 50%?** Industry issue? Company-specific? Temporary?
2. **Is the low P/E justified?** (Low FCF? High declining ROE?)
3. **Is the balance sheet healthy?** (Can survive downturn?)
4. **Are receivables growing?** (Is customer credit okay?)
5. **Is there a catalyst?** (Management change? New product?)

**DANGEROUS patterns:**
- ❌ 50% down + Falling ROE + Rising D/E (Death spiral)
- ❌ 50% down + P/E still 20+ (Market knows something)
- ❌ 50% down + Receivables exploding (Customers defaulting)

**What questions it answers:**
- ✅ Which are genuine deep value opportunities?
- ✅ Which are value traps to avoid?
- ✅ What's the recovery potential?
- ✅ Are fundamentals intact or deteriorating?

**Use case:** Value investing, turnaround strategies, high-risk/reward plays

## 🎲 Practical Investment Workflows

### Workflow 1: **Find Emerging Quality Compounders**

```
1. Check "Start Here" dashboard
   ↓
2. If "Trend Score" is rising → Go to "Trend Analyzer"
   ↓
3. Filter for Trend Score > 20 + Positive Acceleration
   ↓
4. Sort by ROE (Filter > 12%)
   ↓
5. Click company name to check Screener.in for:
   - Management quality
   - 3-year earnings growth
   - Dividend history
   ↓
6. DECISION: Hold/Buy if:
   ✅ Consistent high scores
   ✅ Rising microwave trends
   ✅ Good ROE + Good management
```

### Workflow 2: **Find Near-Term Momentum Trades**

```
1. Go to "Within 5% of 52W High"
   ↓
2. Filter for P/E < 25 (not too expensive)
   ↓
3. Sort by "Δ% MCap" (highest gains since first high)
   ↓
4. Check "Trend Shift" for Δ Hits > 0
   ↓
5. Check "Industry Tailwinds" to confirm sector momentum
   ↓
6. DECISION: Trade if:
   ✅ Multiple confirmations of momentum
   ✅ Sector tailwind
   ✅ MCap growing (not trapped at top)
```

### Workflow 3: **Find Undervalued Recovering Companies**

```
1. Go to "5-50% from 52W High"
   ↓
2. Filter for 20-30% pullback specifically
   ↓
3. Sort by "P/E" (lowest first)
   ↓
4. ADD FILTER:
   - P/BV < 3
   - ROE > 10%
   - D/E < 1.5
   ↓
5. Check if "Δ% MCap" is positive (recovering)
   ↓
6. DECISION: Buy if:
   ✅ Pullback is sector-wide (not company-specific problem)
   ✅ Fundamentals intact or improving
   ✅ Momentum showing signs of recovery
```

### Workflow 4: **Find Deep Value Turnarounds (Advanced)**

```
1. Go to "Big Dippers (50%+ Down)"
   ↓
2. FILTER CHECKLIST (Use all):
   ☑ P/E < 12
   ☑ P/BV < 2
   ☑ ROE > 10%
   ☑ D/E < 1.5
   ☑ Earnings Yield > 8%
   ↓
3. RESEARCH EACH:
   - Click name → Check Screener for catalyst
   - Is there new management?
   - Is there a new product/market?
   - Has competitor landscape changed?
   ↓
4. DECISION: Speculative buy only if:
   ✅ CLEAR catalyst (New CEO, New product, New market)
   ✅ Balance sheet can survive downturn
   ✅ Earnings not collapsing (ROE stable or rising)
   ✅ You can hold 3+ years
```

### Workflow 5: **Diversify Across Sectors**

```
1. Go to "Industry Tailwinds"
   ↓
2. Identify:
   - Top 3 hot sectors
   - Top 3 cold sectors
   ↓
3. Go back to "Momentum Summary"
   ↓
4. Ensure portfolio has representation from:
   - 1-2 hot sectors (Offense)
   - 1-2 average sectors (Core)
   - Avoid cold sectors (Defense)
   ↓
5. DECISION: Rebalance if:
   ✅ One sector dominates (> 40% of holdings)
   ✅ Cold sector rotates hot (Opportunity)
   ✅ Sector trends shift significantly
```

## 🚨 Key Red Flags & Green Flags

###🚩 RED FLAGS (Sell/Avoid)

| Red Flag | Means | Action |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Hits declining over weeks | Losing momentum | Exit the position |
| P/E rising while ROE falling | Getting expensive, less profitable | Don't chase |
| High D/E + Falling earnings | Risky deleveraging needed | Exit |
| Receivables > Sales growing | Customers not paying | Investigate quickly |
| Sector cooling while stock near high | Swimming against tide | Better opportunities elsewhere |
| "Big Dipper" with Col. D/E + Low ROE | Classic value trap | Avoid |
